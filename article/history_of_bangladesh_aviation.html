The History of Commercial Aviation in Bangladesh

  • Towsif
  • Aug 20, 2025
  • 0 Comments

Commercial aviation in Bangladesh has a fascinating journey that reflects the nation’s political changes, economic growth, and aspirations for global connectivity. From modest beginnings during the Pakistan era to a competitive market with multiple airlines today, aviation has played a vital role in shaping Bangladesh’s international presence.

Early Beginnings (Before 1971)

The roots of commercial aviation in present-day Bangladesh go back to the time when it was still East Pakistan. Pakistan International Airlines (PIA) was the dominant carrier, connecting Dhaka with Karachi, Lahore, and international destinations. Dhaka Airport, then known as Tejgaon Airport, served as the main hub for civilian flights. During the 1960s, some smaller regional carriers also operated limited domestic services within East Pakistan, but infrastructure remained underdeveloped.

Birth of Biman Bangladesh Airlines (1972)

Following the Liberation War in 1971, Bangladesh needed its own national airline to represent its sovereignty and connect the country with the outside world. On 4 January 1972, Biman Bangladesh Airlines was established as the national flag carrier. It started operations with a DC-3 aircraft donated by the Indian government, followed by leased planes from other countries. Biman’s first international flight was launched on 4 March 1972, from Dhaka to London, via Calcutta and Delhi.

Through the 1970s and 1980s, Biman expanded its fleet with Fokker F27s, DC-10s, and Airbus A310s, gradually extending its network to the Middle East, Europe, and Asia. However, challenges such as poor management and political interference often limited its efficiency. Growth of Biman Bangladesh Airlines (1972s–present) Biman Bangladesh Airlines, the national flag carrier of Bangladesh, was established on 4 January 1972, shortly after the country’s independence. Its creation marked a crucial step in asserting Bangladesh’s sovereignty and connecting the newly formed nation with the rest of the world. Initially, Biman operated a DC-3 aircraft donated by India and later leased other planes to begin domestic and international flights. Its first international route was launched on 4 March 1972, linking Dhaka with London via Calcutta and Delhi. During the 1970s and 1980s, Biman expanded its fleet to include Fokker F27s, DC-10s, and Airbus A310s, gradually extending services to the Middle East, Europe, and Asia. Despite challenges such as political interference and management inefficiencies, the airline played a pivotal role in promoting Bangladesh internationally.

The 1990s and 2000s brought further growth as Bangladesh’s aviation sector liberalized, though private airlines began to emerge, creating competition. Biman responded by modernizing its fleet and focusing on maintaining key international connections, especially in the Middle East.

In the 2010s to present, Biman has significantly upgraded its operations. Its fleet now includes Boeing 777s, 737-800s, and Dreamliners (787-8 and 787-9). The airline has improved passenger services, expanded its international destinations, and strengthened its position as the country’s leading carrier. Today, Biman Bangladesh Airlines is not only a symbol of national pride but also a key player in Bangladesh’s growing aviation industry, serving millions of passengers annually and contributing to the nation’s connectivity and economy. Growth of Private Airlines (1990s–2000s) The 1990s marked the liberalization of Bangladesh’s aviation industry. The government allowed private airlines to operate alongside Biman, leading to the rise of several carriers. Notable early entrants included GMG Airlines (1998), which became Bangladesh’s first private airline to operate international flights. Other airlines such as Air Parabat, United Airways, and Best Air also emerged, though many struggled to survive in the competitive market due to financial and operational challenges.

Modern Era: Competition and Expansion (2010s–Present)

Since the 2010s, Bangladesh’s aviation industry has entered a new era with the entry of modern private airlines:

US-Bangla Airlines (2014)

US-Bangla Airlines is the largest private airline in Bangladesh. It operates a fleet of Boeing 737-800s and ATR 72-600s, serving both domestic and international routes. Known for its punctuality and reliable service, the airline has become especially popular on flights to the Middle East and South Asia, supporting the vast Bangladeshi expatriate community.

Novoair (2013)

It is a Bangladeshi private airline that started operations in January 2013 with small Embraer ERJ-145 jets. Later, it transitioned to ATR 72-500 turboprop aircraft, gradually expanding its fleet to 7 at its peak. At present (2025), Novoair operates 5 ATR 72-500s, serving mainly domestic routes along with limited international services. Known for punctuality and service quality, the airline carries the slogan “The Art of Aviation.”

Regent Airways (2010–2020)

IT was a Bangladeshi private airline launched in 2010 under the Habib Group with the slogan “Every Little Thing Counts.” It started operations with two Dash-8-Q300 turboprop aircraft and later expanded with Boeing 737-700 and 737-800 jets, growing its fleet to 8 aircraft at its peak. The airline operated both domestic and international routes before ceasing operations in 2020.

Air Astra (2021)

It is a privately owned Bangladeshi airline headquartered at Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka. Established in 2021, it started operations on November 24, 2022, becoming the first private airline in Bangladesh in nearly nine years to receive an Air Operator Certificate (AOC). The airline currently operates a fleet of four ATR 72-600 aircraft, with plans to expand to ten by 2025. Air Astra focuses on enhancing domestic connectivity and is preparing to launch international flights targeting high-demand markets.
